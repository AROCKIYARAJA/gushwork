HDPE Pipe Technical Specification Report

The High-Density Polyethylene (HDPE) pipes are engineered to meet diverse industrial and infrastructure requirements with a wide diameter range from 20mm to 1600mm (3/4" to 63"). These pipes are available in multiple pressure ratings, including PN 2.5, PN 4, PN 6, PN 8, PN 10, PN 12.5, and PN 16, ensuring suitability for varying pressure conditions.

To accommodate different strength and thickness requirements, the pipes are manufactured in several Standard Dimension Ratios (SDR) such as SDR 33, SDR 26, SDR 21, SDR 17, SDR 13.6, and SDR 11. They are capable of operating efficiently within a wide temperature range of -40°C to +80°C (-40°F to +176°F), making them reliable in extreme environmental conditions.

Under standard operating conditions (20°C, PN 10), the pipes offer a service life exceeding 50 years, ensuring long-term durability and cost-effectiveness. The material used has a density of 0.95–0.96 g/cm³, providing an optimal balance between strength and lightweight properties.

These HDPE pipes comply with recognized international standards, including IS 5984, ISO 4427, and ASTM D3035, guaranteeing quality, safety, and performance consistency. They support multiple jointing methods such as butt fusion, electrofusion, and mechanical joints, allowing flexibility in installation.

For ease of transportation and handling, especially in smaller diameters, the pipes are available in coil lengths of up to 500 meters. The product is manufactured in India 🇮🇳, adhering to stringent quality control measures.
